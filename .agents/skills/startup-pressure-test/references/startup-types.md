# Startup Type Lenses

Apply the relevant lens when the startup type is clear. Use only the sections needed for the task.

## B2B SaaS

Check:

- Buyer vs user split.
- Budget owner and urgency.
- Existing spend or painful manual process.
- Integration, security, and procurement friction.
- Sales cycle relative to founder resources.
- Expansion path after the wedge.

High-risk pattern: nice dashboard with no budget owner or workflow ownership.

## Consumer

Check:

- Frequency of use.
- Emotional intensity or status/identity pull.
- Retention after novelty fades.
- Distribution channel before paid ads.
- Monetization that does not kill growth.

High-risk pattern: broad lifestyle app with weak habit and expensive acquisition.

## Marketplace

Check:

- Which side is constrained.
- Why either side joins before liquidity exists.
- Trust, safety, quality, and matching.
- Frequency of transactions.
- Whether a manual brokered version can work first.

High-risk pattern: needing both sides at scale before value exists.

## Devtool

Check:

- Developer pain that occurs repeatedly.
- Time saved or failure avoided.
- Integration surface and migration cost.
- Open-source, bottoms-up, or community distribution.
- Pricing basis: seat, usage, team, infra savings, or enterprise.

High-risk pattern: clever tool with no adoption path into real team workflows.

## AI Workflow Tool

Check:

- Whether the value is workflow ownership, not just model access.
- Whether output quality can be measured.
- Data access, permissions, and integration depth.
- Human review burden and failure cost.
- Whether incumbents can add the feature easily.

High-risk pattern: thin wrapper where distribution and data remain with another platform.

## Agency Or Service

Check:

- Whether the founder can deliver manually now.
- Margin and repeatability.
- Which steps can become product later.
- Whether customers buy outcomes or software.
- Whether service revenue creates learning or distraction.

High-risk pattern: calling a bespoke service "SaaS" before the workflow repeats.

## Regulated, Fintech, Health, Legal, Or Education

Check:

- Compliance, liability, privacy, and trust.
- Buyer conservatism and procurement.
- Data sensitivity.
- Whether the first wedge can avoid regulated scope.
- Need for expert review or licensed operators.

High-risk pattern: treating regulation as an implementation detail.

## Hardware Or Physical Operations

Check:

- Manufacturing, logistics, support, and inventory risk.
- Capital requirements before validation.
- Failure modes in the field.
- Unit economics and replacement cycles.
- Whether a manual or software-only test can validate demand first.

High-risk pattern: hardware build before demand proof.

## Local Or Service Business

Check:

- Geographic density.
- Repeat purchase or referral loop.
- Operational complexity.
- Local acquisition channels.
- Whether software meaningfully improves delivery or sales.

High-risk pattern: trying to make a local operations business look venture-scale without density or network effects.
