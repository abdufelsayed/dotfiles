# Research Protocol

Use this file when competitor, market, pricing, platform, regulation, or model-capability facts materially affect the answer.

## When To Research

Research if:

- The user asks for competitors, market map, pricing, or current tools.
- The idea depends on a fast-changing technology, regulation, or platform.
- A named competitor, market leader, or distribution channel affects the verdict.
- The answer would otherwise require guessing at current facts.

Skip research if:

- The user asks for a pure internal gut-check.
- The needed judgment is about logic, assumptions, or validation design.
- Tools are unavailable. In that case, state what must be verified.

## Source Priority

Prefer:

- Official competitor websites, docs, changelogs, and pricing pages.
- Product directories and marketplaces.
- Review sites for customer complaints and positioning.
- Forums, communities, social posts, and issue trackers for current pain.
- Regulatory or government sources for compliance claims.

Use secondary articles cautiously and note when claims are not primary evidence.

## Search Patterns

Adapt queries to the idea:

- `[customer] [workflow] software`
- `[pain] tool for [customer]`
- `[competitor] pricing`
- `[competitor] alternatives`
- `site:g2.com [category]`
- `site:reddit.com [pain] [customer]`
- `site:github.com [problem] [developer tool]`
- `[regulation/platform/model] latest change`

## Output Rules

- Cite links when research was used.
- Separate observed facts from inferences.
- Do not invent market size, revenue, user count, funding, or pricing.
- If evidence is mixed, say what is mixed.
- If only weak sources exist, make the uncertainty visible.

## Competitor Mapping

For each meaningful competitor or alternative, capture:

- Who it serves.
- What workflow it owns.
- Pricing or buying motion if visible.
- The switching cost.
- The customer's likely reason to stay.
- The wedge required to displace it.
